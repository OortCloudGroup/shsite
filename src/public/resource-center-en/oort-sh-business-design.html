Why

Why keep developing an application enablement platform?

Because a platform is not a fixed name, but an evolving tool system for real needs. Needs persist over the long term; products are only solutions for a specific phase, scenario, and technical condition.

After four years, the application enablement platform has completed at most 20% of the original vision. But this is not a failure. It shows that the real problem is much bigger than any single product form: products are never unique or permanent, while the needs remain.

The value of a tool is not in its name, but in the feeling of holding it in your hand: whether it is easy to use, saves effort, and helps business systems become runnable faster.

Best Practices

Best practices for designing business software: production-oriented from the start

AI makes generation faster, while OORT.sh makes landing steadier. True best practice is to design requirements, engineering, deployment, and operations on the same map.

Do not design pages first; design business objects first

Clarify people, events, objects, organizations, processes, states, permissions, and constraints before letting pages carry these business structures.

Do not turn every project into an island

Identify shared capabilities such as authentication, notifications, approvals, files, logs, tasks, and reports early, and prioritize accumulating them as services.

AI-generated code must enter engineering standards

Bring AI-generated results into directory structures, API specifications, permission models, configuration management, testing, and deployment pipelines.

Plan installation and deployment before launch

Production-oriented design must include environment variables, dependent services, data migration, upgrades, rollback, and private delivery.

All services must be observable and governable

Logs, metrics, alerts, audits, access control, and runtime boundaries are the foundation for long-term availability of business systems.
