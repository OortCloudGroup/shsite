Template Modes

Two template types: integrated project templates and separate deployment templates

Both are templates, but their applicable stages and engineering logic are completely different: the former helps quickly start a complete business project, while the latter supports more complex, long-term, and specialized system evolution.

01

Project Template

A project template places frontend, backend services, database, infrastructure, deployment scripts, configuration, and engineering conventions in one project. It is ideal for quickly generating a complete runnable project from 0 to 1.

  • Unified repository, dependencies, and startup commands reduce initial collaboration costs.
  • Built-in infrastructure descriptions such as database, cache, object storage, gateway, and CI/CD.
  • Suitable for prototype validation, small and medium-sized businesses, customer-specific projects, and teaching demos.
Single ProjectReady to UseFull-Stack LoopRapid Delivery
02

Separate Deployment Template

Other template types focus more on advanced separate deployment logic: frontend, services, agents, data, GIS, training jobs, low-code engines, and more evolve independently by domain and combine into business systems through platform capabilities.

  • Each template maps to a specialized capability domain and can be independently released, scaled, and governed.
  • Suitable for large systems, platform products, multi-team collaboration, and long-term operations.
  • Composable capabilities are formed through APIs, events, permissions, data contracts, and service catalogs.
